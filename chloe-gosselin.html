Early Life 

Chloe Gosselin was born on August 22, 1984, in Dreux, France. The details of her parents and siblings, however, are unclear. Chloe Gosselin attended the prestigious Fine Arts Programme at the School of Lacambre in Belgium. After working as an international model for ten years, Gosselin went back to school to study shoe manufacture and design at Ars Sutoria in Milan and the Fashion Institute of Technology in New York, where her company was founded.

Gosselin’s love of modern art, design, music, and precious antiques produces a collection that is not trend-driven. She quickly discovered a void in the luxury shoe industry for classic, subtle styles that are appropriate for the contemporary woman and all facets of her everyday life.

Chloe Gosselin Career

The 2014-founded Chloe Gosselin premium shoe company expertly fuses Italian craftsmanship with French style. Each pair of shoes is manufactured by hand in a secluded town a few miles outside of Florence after being developed in her New York office. Classic design, the best materials, and great craftsmanship are prioritized, together with a distinct youthfulness and playful style.

Her characteristic shapes and instantly recognizable brand image have evolved as a result of distinctive elements including the traditional single-sole sandal, corset ties, and curved paneling. The types of fabrics include alluring skins, velvet, and suede. The best-selling ‘Larkspur’, a single-soled sandal is a modern spin on the universal open-toed seasonal offering and has become an iconic design.

Previously identified as a ‘brand to watch,’ Chloe Gosselin’s ability to consistently offer sought-after collections and timeless designs has cemented her position at the forefront of the global luxury footwear market. Chloe is a finalist for the 2016 CFDA/Vogue Fashion Fund.
