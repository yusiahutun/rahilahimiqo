2. Offshore drilling rig manager

A rig manager is an experienced and highly skilled professional conducting offshore drilling rig operations. Offshore drilling rigs are huge structures used to drill for gas and oil in offshore waters far from the coastline.

The individual requires a high school diploma and extensive experience working in a rigged environment. The salary for an offshore drilling rig manager ranges from $92,228 to $118,210 annually. A rig manager performs duties such as:

  • Ensuring that the permit-to-work system and HSE management tools are used correctly.
  • Conduct pre-tour meetings with rig crews.
  • Coordinating rig moves with the client and transportation personnel.
  • Managing the set-up and take-down of rigs
  • Coordinating rig crews.
  • Resolving operational and personnel issues with rig crews and contractors
