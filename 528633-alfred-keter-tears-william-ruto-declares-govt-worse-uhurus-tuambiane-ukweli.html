Alfred Keter Tears Into William Ruto Again, Declares His Govt Worse than Uhuru's: &quot;Tuambiane Ukweli&quot;

  • Keter warned Rift Valley residents against supporting the Kenya Kwanza administration blindly
  • The outspoken politician insisted that former president Uhuru Kenyatta's administration was far better than the current government

Amos Khaemba, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings over three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Nandi - Former Nandi Hills MP Alfred Keter has once again criticised President William Ruto's Kenya Kwanza administration.

Addressing mourners over the weekend, the vocal politician accused the Kenya Kwanza government of failing to deal with the country's economic challenges.

Keter, who is never afraid of speaking his mind, said tribalism had denied the country the chance to elect competent leaders.

Alfred Keter: Why Ruto's government is the worst

According to Keter, Ruto's administration had made life more difficult by introducing oppressive taxes and policies.

"The problem in this country is tribalism; you want to have your own, and they don't fix issues. Take it to the bank; come January, most students will not go to school because school fees have been hiked three times. Very few people will celebrate this Christmas," Keter said.

Keter told Rift Valley residents not to blindly support the Kenya Kwanza government, arguing that they would suffer like any other Kenyans despite Ruto being their kingpin.

The fearless politician went ahead to claim that former president Uhuru Kenyatta was better compared to Ruto.

"Petrol stations do not have those in government and opposition. If you are a sufferer, you are just a sufferer. In my view, this Kenya Kwanza government is worse than Uhuru's government. Because Uhuru left fuel at KSh 140; it's now at KSh 210," he stated.

Alfred Keter says Ruto is becoming unpopular

As reported earlier, the outspoken former Nandi Hills lawmaker castigated President Ruto's leadership style.

In a statement, the outspoken former MP said the Kenya Kwanza administration was becoming unpopular at an alarming rate.

According to the former lawmaker, the move by a section of Kericho County Assembly Members (MCAs) to walk out on the president shows all was not well.

"Ruto goes to Kericho County (Rift Valley) to open a Kibaki regime water project, Kericho MCAs walked out in protest. This wheelbarrow administration is becoming very unpopular at an alarming rate. All trees are slippery on the day the monkey is destined to die," he stated.
