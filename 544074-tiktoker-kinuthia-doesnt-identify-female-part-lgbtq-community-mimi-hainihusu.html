Popular crossdresser Kelvin Kinuthia has set the record straight regarding his sexuality.

Kinuthia and the LGBTQ+ community

Speaking to YouTuber Mungai Eve, Kinuthia urged people to mind their business, saying that discussions around the LGBTQ community have nothing to do with him.

According to the TikToker, everybody has a right to live their lives and identify however they want.

Addressing a direct inquiry about his association with the LGBTQ community, Kinuthia maintained that his personal life, including his sexuality and romantic preferences, remains his private affair.

He disclosed that his sexuality and dating preferences are purely his business.

"Nilipata watu wakiuliza but hiyo ni swali awkward...if you are, you are...hiyo ni story yako mimi hainihusu," he said."Kama niko it's my life, kama siko it's my life pia," he added.

Just call me Kinuthia

In another section of the interview, Kinuthia also touched upon the discomfort he experiences when people address him as a man, particularly when he's fully adorned in women's clothing.

Although he said he did not find it particularly offensive, he prefers it if they call him by his name instead.

"There was a time you said that when you meet with your male fans out there, they should stop calling you, bro. You said that you identify as baby girl...why did you feel the urge to tell them, 'be calling me baby girl'?" Eve asked.

Kinuthia responded:

"Siwezisema ni offended, inakaanga awkward..imagine umeng'ara uko katikati ya mall halafu mtu anashout, niaje bro'...I wouldn't say nafindigi ikiwanga mbaya, nafindigi tu ikiwa awkward."

When it comes to pronouns, Kinuthia is flexible, leaving the decision to his fans' discretion.

"You, as an individual, do you like to identify as he or she? Ama you don't have a problem when it comes to the pronouns?" Eve asked."Watu hushindwa wataniitaje. Just call me Kinuthia...ukitaka kuniita 'he' tumia 'he' ukitaka kuniita 'she' tumia 'she'," Kinuthia replied.
