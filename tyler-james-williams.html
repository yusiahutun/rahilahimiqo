Professional Career

Early Years

At the age of 4, Tyler James Williams began his acting career. As a voice actor, he debuted on the television show called ‘Little Bill’ portraying the role of Bobby, briefly replacing Devon Malik Beckford in 2000.

Later, he portrayed his character in the children’s television series ‘Sesame Street,’ from 2000 to 2005. His work as a young actor was praised, and after a recurring part in the television show, he was offered the main role in the series ‘Everybody Hates Chris.’ From the television series, he rose to prominence.

Raised to Fame

In the 2006 film ‘The Ant Bully,’ he voices a blue teammate. After completing the last season of ‘Everybody Hates Chris,’ in 2009, he appeared in the TV shows ‘The Cleaner’ and ‘True Jackson, VP.’

In the 2006 Christmas comedy film ‘Unaccomplished Minors,’ he played Charles Goldfinch, also known as Charlie in the UK and Ireland. Even though the film did not do well, his performance in it was recognized.

With his appearance in ‘Batman: The Brave and the Bold’ in 2010, he established himself as a talented voice actor. His television film ‘Let It, Shine,’ was a huge success in 2012.

He not only gave an outstanding performance, but he also used his musical skills in musical drama.  The movie won positive reviews from critics and the media.

As a result, he earned another prominent part in the sitcom ‘Go On’ as Owen Lewis and guest appeared in ‘Lab Rats’ alongside his younger brother Tyrel Jackson. In 2013, he appeared as Simon Peeples in Tina Gordon Chism’s American comedy movie, ‘Peeples.’

The movie grossed to earn over .3 million at the box office. He appeared in Justin Simien’s satirical comedy film ‘Dear White People’ in 2014 and was cast as the film’s lead.

The film was all about racial issues and inequality as seen through the perspective of Afro-Americans. It performed well at the box office, making more than 4.6 million.

Other movies and series appearances

He also appeared as ‘Noah’ in a recurring role in AMC’s popular horror drama ‘The Walking Dead’ in 2014. In the fifth season of the show, he appeared in ten episodes.

Later, in 2016, he auditioned for and was cast as ‘Russ Montgomery,’ also known as ‘Monty,’ in the police drama series ‘Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders.’ The show lasted one season and thirteen episodes.

He portrayed the role of Jae in the 2019 movie The Wedding Year, a teen romantic comedy film directed by Robert Luketic and starring Sarah Hyland, Jenna Dewan, Matt Shively, and Anna Camp.

Williams portrayed Paul in the American drama film, The Argument in 2020. In 2021, he appeared as Gregory Eddie in the ABC Mockumentary sitcom Abbott Elementary.

Awards

As a well-known actor, he has collected a few awards and nominations in his acting career. In 2015, Williams earned the ‘Best Breakthrough Performance,’ for his role in the Hollywood film ‘Dear White People,’ at the ‘Black Reel Award.’

Similarly, he earned the ‘Outstanding Actor in a Comedy Series for his role in the sitcom, ‘Everybody Hates Chris,’ at the 2007 NAACP Image Award. In 2008, he earned Best Performance in a TV Series – Leading Young Actor for his role in the sitcom, ‘Everybody Hates Chris,’ at the Young Artist Awards.

Moreover, he earned Outstanding Independent Motion Picture at the 2018 NAACP Image Award. In 2022, he was nominated for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series, Abbott Elementary.
