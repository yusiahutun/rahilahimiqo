Which country has the heaviest buildings? It would be fascinating to know the nation with the heaviest structural masterpieces, built for different purposes. 1. The Palace of Parliament in Bucharest

The Palace of Parliament in Bucharest is believed to be the heaviest building in the world. It was built from 700,000 tonnes of steel and bronze, combined with a massive 1 million m³ (35.3 million ft³) of marble, 3,500 tonnes (7.7 million lb) of crystal glass and 900,000 m³ (31.7 million ft³) of wood.

Construction of the building started in 1984. More than 40,000 people had to be relocated before construction began. According to estimates, more than 30,000 workers worked on building the palace, many working day and night. Another exciting thing about the famous building is that Romania had to increase its export to repay loans for the construction.

2. Pyramid of Khufu

Another massive structure is the Pyramid of Khufu which appears to have been constructed with impossibly heavy stones. And that is why the pyramids are part of the world's list of wonders, as it is hard to imagine how they did it all those years ago with the simple tools they possessed.

3. NASA vehicle assembly building

The NASA assembly building is another addition to the list of heaviest buildings in the world. The megastructure is utilised for the construction of rockets and is quite spacious. It covers around 8 acres and took 98,590 tons of steel to construct, making it one of the heaviest buildings globally.

4. Great Mosque of Mecca

One thing about mosques is that they form the most significant share of the enormous structures in the world as they try to make something marvellous with every construction. The Great Mosque of Mecca is massive, holding up to 4 million people. The building stands on 99 acres of land, which means it is more than 4.9 million square feet. Its construction consumed over 850,000 tons.

6. The New Century Global Centre

How big is the New Century Global Centre in China? The building is massive and is almost 19 million square feet. This gigantic structure required massive input, including tons of concrete and steel. It weighs nearly 2.3 million tons.

7. The Pentagon

The Pentagon is another massive building in the United States of America located in Arlington, VA. The building is almost 4 million square feet and uses nearly 30 acres. Building it required around 700,000 tons of sand from the nearby Potomac River.

8. Taipei 101

The Taipei 101 is a humungous structure weighing 240,000 tons. It consists of 101 floors (hence the 101 in its name). The building was constructed with a 728-ton damper.

9. Lotte World Tower

The Lotte World Tower is a 123-storey skyscraper in, Songpa-gu, Seoul, South Korea. It is the sixth-largest building in the world and the tallest in the OECD countries as of 2017.

The roof structure of the building was made to withstand its weight without reinforcing pillars, sustain earthquakes up to a magnitude of 9 under the Richter magnitude scale, and winds up to 80 m/s. The building weighs over 750,000 tons.

10. Makkah Clock Tower

The Makkah Clock Tower stands at 601 meters (1972 feet) and is the third tallest building in the world. It is also the world’s fourth tallest freestanding structure. Building the tower cost a whopping $15 billion. The tower is located in Saudi Arabia, and it weighs 36,000 tons and contains 76 elevators.

11. The Windsor Castle

Windsor Castle is one of the oldest buildings on the list, as it was initially built in the late 11th century to be a fortress. It later became the home of the royal family.

12. St. Peter’s Basilica

What do you think St. Peter’s Basilica in the Vatican weighs, considering its massive size? The megastructure covers 4,200,000 square feet and weighs more than 500,000 tons. It was built in 1626 and is regarded as one of the greatest masterpieces of architecture of its time.

13. Basilica of the National Shrine of Our Lady of Aparecida

Another massive structure is the Basilica of the National Shrine of Our Lady of Aparecida, which is also one of the largest churches in the world. The church is located in Aparecida, Brazil. It has a capacity of 45,000 people and can accommodate up to 350,000 during outdoor services.

The Torre Mayor is a massive Mexico City building with 225 metres at the top floor and 55 stories. Its gross floor area is 1,687,103 square feet. The building is made with premium materials to withstand earthquakes common in Mexico City.

In 2003, a 7.6 magnitude shook Mexico City, and the building felt nothing. Also, the occupants inside did not even know an earthquake had occurred.

15. The First World Hotel

The First World Hotel is immense in Pahang, Malaysia, with 7,351 rooms. It holds the record as the biggest hotel in the world. The building houses numerous restaurants, a shopping mall and a 500,000 square feet theme park.
