For his next role, Duchovny stars in Pet Sematary: Bloodlines, which is scheduled to premiere at Fantastic Fest in September 2023.

The new supernatural horror film, directed and co-written by Lindsey Anderson Beer, will then be released on Paramount+ on October 6.

In the coming months Duchovny will also be rehearsing with his band, ahead of their 2023 European Tour, which kicks off on November 6 with a show in Edinburgh, Scotland.

So far, their trek consists of 10 gigs with stops in England, Germany, France, Netherlands, Austria and Italy.

Duchovny and his backing band have released three studio albums since 2015, with the latest being Gestureland (2021).

His sound has been described as a mixture of folks rock, alternative rock and country, according to various publications, including USA Today.

Self-taught on the guitar, the Hollywood leading man and frontman only sings on Gestureland.

'I don't have a great melodic voice or perfect pitch. I don't have any of those things that one associates with a great singer but I keep trying to get it right,' he confessed during an interview following the record's release. 'But now that I've recorded more and played live a bunch, it cures you of any preciousness of hitting notes smack dab in the middle. I have no compunction about getting in a (vocal) booth right now and trying to sing a song, but now I know I'm capable and push myself.'

'Finally going to get to play some of Gestureland live for you,' Duchovny wrote in an Instagram post when announcing the European Tour.  See you in November.'
