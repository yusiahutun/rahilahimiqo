Sammy Boy: Gospel Singer Serving Life Sentence for Having Stolen Items Maintains Innocence

May 16, 2024
blog
  • A decision to host a trusted friend for a few days was all it took to turn the life of renowned Kikuyu Gospel artiste Sammy Boy upside down
  • According to him, the friend visited him with stolen items he was unaware of until the cops showed up at his rented house
  • Sammy's case became complicated when the young man died in a road accident, which saw him imprisoned for robbery with violence

Renowned Kikuyu gospel artiste Sammy Boy is serving a life sentence at Naivasha Maximum Prison over theft charges, something he claims he is innocent of.

According to the No Ira Tu crooner, he was convicted over what was initially meant to be a favour to a trusted friend, like anyone else would.

Hosted a friend

Sammy says that his woes started when he agreed to host a trusted friend from the countryside who came over with a piece of luggage.

Given that he was hosting the individual for a few days, he did not bother to inquire into the whereabouts of the items.

Unknown to him, the luggage in his rented house contained stolen goods that were soon traced by the authorities leading to his arrest.

Convicted for robbery with violence

As it happened, the friend disappeared during the same period and left him to face the law despite him not knowing what was happening.

What Sammy thought was a simple case once the friend was apprehended to serve his rightful sentence became complicated when the latter died in a tragic accident.

"With the would-be suspect out of the picture, the charges were pinned on me. Since I did not have a lawyer, the court convicted me for life," he said.

Everything started falling apart for him after the conviction as he learnt not long after that his wife had divorced him.

Court appeal ongoing

Although he feels unfairly imprisoned, Sammy has since settled in prison and is currently a teacher to fellow inmates.

He is, however, working round the clock to secure a court appeal that will hopefully overturn the conviction and hand him his freedom back.

"By the mercies of God, I will one day be set free," said the optimistic crooner.

He also hopes to secure the release early enough so that he can put the dark period behind him and rebuild his life.

Sad story of Anne Makena

Sammy's story brings back memories of 22-year-old Anne Makena who was sentenced for a crime she had no idea about.

Makena disclosed that her boss stocked contraband alcohol at the wines and spirits she was employed but was not informed, only for KEBS officers to storm in and arrest her.

She was released shortly after her story went viral and has been undergoing counselling for the psychological trauma she currently lives with.
