DJ Mustard- Biography

DJ Mustard, a record producer, songwriter, disc jockey, and record executive, is well-known in Dijon. He is an American who was born in California. His music is generally acclaimed by the young people that follow him on social media. His popular YouTube channel has 1.3 million subscribers.

This 31-year-old personality was born in 1990 and is a dynamo of energy and vitality. His songs are mostly in the hip-hop and R&B genres. He frequently collaborates with other well-known artists from California, like YG and Ty Dolla Sign. His iconic “Rack City” with Tyga in 2011 got him famous.

Mustard is well-known for its distinctive sound, which includes gorgeous, vibrant, and party-type music with a melodic hip-hop tone. He refers to it as Ratchet music, and it has subsequently gained prominence in the music industry. Another distinguishing feature of his productions is that they usually end or begin with the tag “Mustard on the beat, yo.”Mustard has made a reputation for himself in the business with three single albums: 10 Summers in 2014, Cold Summer in 2016, and Perfect Ten in 2019.

DJ Mustard- Professional Career

Mustard was interested in music as a child and tried his hand at it with his uncle. He began with family and social gatherings and then considered making it a career choice. He collaborated on mixtapes with his friend and fellow rapper YG. Fortunately, it resulted in success, and other artists began to take notice of him.

Ratchet Music, his music creation, gave him recognition and acclaim. Rack City, which peaked at number seven on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, I’m Different, Headband, My Nigga, and Show Me were all significant hits. Mustard’s debut album, 10 Summers, was released in 2014 and peaked at number 14 on the US Rap chart. Furthermore, his second album Cold Summer, released in 2016, reached the 14th spot on the US Rap chart.

Feelin’ Myself, which included Miley Cyrus, French Montana, Wiz Khalifa, 2 On, which featured Schoolboy Q, Main Chick, which featured Chris Brown, and many more, was another of Mustard’s successful song productions.
