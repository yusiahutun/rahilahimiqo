Shonda Rhimes Bio & Age

Shonda Lynn Rhimes was born in Chicago, Illinois, on January 13, 1970, the daughter of Vera and Ille Rhimes Jr. She was the youngest of six children. Her father was a college professor, while her mother was a university administrator.

Her mother Vera attended college while raising all of her children and earning a doctorate. Her father later became the Chief Information Officer of the University of Southern California. She lived with her parents and siblings in Park Forest South, Illinois.

From an early age, she showed an affinity for storytelling. During her high school years, she volunteered at the hospital, which sparked her interest in the hospital environment. She attended Marian Catholic High School in Illinois and later Dartmouth College, where she studied English and Film Studies.

During her school years, she joined the Black Underground Theater Association, which meant that she divided her time between directing, performing, and writing. She graduated from Dartmouth in 1991.

After college, she moved to San Francisco and began working for McCann Erickson, a global advertising company. Shortly thereafter she moved to Los Angeles and began studying screenwriting at the University of Southern California, where she was top of her class and was awarded the Gary Rosenberg Writing Fellowship.

She received her master’s degree from the USC School of Cinematic Arts. While still a student at USC, she began an internship with Debra Martin Chase, a film and television producer who served as Shonda’s mentor.
