William Ruto Cautions Pastors Against Using Religion for Selfish Interests: &quot;Shetani Amekasirika&quot;

  • This came as the death toll from the Shakahola cult surpassed the 200 mark as the exhumation process gained momentum

President William Ruto has cautioned Kenyan religious leaders against using religion for selfish interests and has further promised dire consequences for those abusing the sanctity of the Christian faith.

Coming against the backdrop of the shocking Shakahola deaths as the toll surpassed the 200 mark, the president said pastors should desist from using Biblical teachings to extort or manipulate the masses.

Ruto pledges thorough probe

Speaking earlier today at AIC Milimani, the first in command said his government is working to ensure that the Shakahola cult has been investigated to determine what happened to the victims.

"We have to be sensitive, and I know that our religious leaders will advise us on how true religious will remain protected from the people who have aims that are not very legitimate," he said as reported by Citizen TV.Our security agencies are working around the clock so that we can unravel these acts that shame the people who want to abuse the sanctity of religion and cause loss of lives," he added.

The head of state said he will ensure that such destruction will never be witnessed again on Kenyan soil.

Shakahola Commission of inquiry

As reported, a few weeks ago, Ruto appointed the Shakahola Massacre Commission of Inquiry to get to the bottom of the incident.

The commission's mandate is to inquire into the deaths, torture, and inhumane and degrading treatment of members and individuals linked to Pastor Paul Mackenzie-led Good News International Church in Malindi, Kilifi county.

The commissioners are Justice (Rtd) Mary Kasango, Frank Njenga (psychiatrist), Eric Gumbo, Bishop Catherine Mutua, Jonathan Lodompui, Wanyama Musiambu, and Albert Musasia.

According to the gazette notice, the Commission of Inquiry will prepare a report and submit its recommendation to Ruto within six months.

Yesterday, the death toll crossed 200 and exhumation was momentarily halted due to logistical reasons. The process is expected to resume on Tuesday.
