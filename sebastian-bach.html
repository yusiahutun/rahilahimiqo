Sebastian Bach is a well-known professional Canadian singer and songwriter. Sebastian Bach came on the rise as the frontman of the popular American rock band, Skid Row, from 1987 to 1996.

Early Life and Childhood

Sebastian Bach was born Sebastian Philip Bierk on 3rd April 1968. His birth took place in Freeport, Bahamas, Canada. Therefore, he holds Canadian citizenship and is currently 55 years old. Likewise, he follows Christianity and his birth sign is Aries.

Moving on to his family members, he was born to his father David Bierk, and his mother Kathleen Mae Hunter. His father was a renowned painter and also painted one of the album covers of his band, Skid Row.

Moving on, Bach has seven siblings. His brother Zac Bierk is a professional ice hockey player and Jeff Bierk is a photographer. Also, his sister Dylan Bierk is an actress.

Likewise, his other brothers Alex, Charles, and Nick were also professional painters. Sebastian was raised in Peterborough City, Ontario, Canada.

Education

In terms of his educational status, he attended the Lakefield College School in Ontario. The rockstar had a strong inclination toward rock music since his childhood.

Professional Life and Career

Sebastian started his music career at the age of 14. He started playing in the band named “Kid Wikkid”. Similarly, he moved to Toronto and started living with his aunt to pursue his career with the band.

However, the band separated in 1985. After that, he joined the American band “Madam X” but left soon because of an unpleasant experience.

In 1987, he joined another band, “Skid Row” and filled up the vacant space of lead singer, Matt Fallon. Sebastian got his breakthrough after his performance at rock photographer Mark’s wedding. In January 1989, the band released its first album, “Skid Row”.

The album was a hit with popular songs, “I Remember You” and “18 and Life”. Their next album titled, “Slave to the Grind” was released in June 1991.

However, in 1996, he got fired from the band due to conflicts with the members. Moreover, he formed his band named “The Last Hard Men” in 1996.

In September 2011, Sebastian released his next solo album named “Kicking & Screaming” and also released “Give ‘Em Hell” in 2014. On April 12, 2016, Dey Street Books published a book on him titled, “18 And Life On Skid Row” which won the best book of the year in 2017.
