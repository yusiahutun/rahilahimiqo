Love Island 2023 is set to kick off on Monday night with a brand new batch of sexy singletons entering the famous Mallorca villa.

Among those making their debut is communications manager Mehdi Edno, who is hoping to find his dream girl when he arrives on the show.

Bosses have already revealed that this summer viewers will have more control than ever, as they vote which girls and boys will be coupled up right from the start of the series.

The French hunk admitted he was convinced to sign up for the show by his friends after being single for two years.

So, as Love Island fans tune in for eight weeks of romance and drama, here is everything you need to know about Mehdi Edno...

Who is Love Island's Mehdi Edno? Age, job, Instagram and nationality revealed

Who is Mehdi Edno?

Mehdi, 26, is a hunky French model, originally from Bordeax but now residing in London.

He is said to be 'the definition of tall, dark and handsome' and he certainly isn't shy about flaunting his looks on social media. 

When asked if her was  ready to find ‘the one’, Mehdi said: Yes. I’ve been busy for the last few years with work, and doing my Masters degree but now I’m done with the busy schedule, and ready to find love.

Revealing what he does to get somebody to date him, he added: 'I’d simply start by speaking French, that usually gets girls interested', while he also listed what's important for him in a partner. 

He said: 'Looks are important but they aren’t everything, a good sense of humour and someone who is outgoing and up for an adventure is what I’m looking for.'

Producers are thought to be hoping that Mehdi will be the next Davide Sancliment, after the Italian Stallion won the last series with Ekin-Su Cülcüloğlu.

When will Love Island 2023 begin?

A statement released by the show confirmed that Love Island will return to Mallorca on Monday June 5 at 9pm on ITV2 and ITVX.

The show will once again air every night from Sundays to Fridays, with an Unseen Bits show broadcast on Saturday nights.

As well as tuning in nightly, viewers will be able to watch, and catch up, on the series so far on ITVX.

Plus, returning to ITV2 and streaming on ITVX is Love Island: Aftersun, with Maya Jama hosting live from London each Sunday evening.

Also set to make its return this summer is the official Love Island podcast, Love Island: The Morning After, hosted by Sam Thomspon and Indiyah Polak.
