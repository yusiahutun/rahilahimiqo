Chance Perdomo, 27, dies in fatal motorcycle crash weeks after 'celebrating' new vehicle

July 11, 2024
blog

Chance Perdomo, known for his role in Gen V, has tragically died in a motorbike crash aged 27.

The TV star appeared in several shows including Netflix‘s horror, Chilling Adventures of Sabrina, where he played Ambrose Spellman for four seasons.

Chance Perdomo dies aged 27 in tragic motorcycle crash

“It is with heavy hearts that we share the news of Chance Perdomo’s untimely passing as a result of a motorcycle accident,” the star’s publicist told Variety.

“His passion for the arts and insatiable appetite for life was felt by all who knew him, and his warmth will carry on in those who he loved dearest.

We ask to please respect the family’s wish for privacy as they mourn the loss of their beloved son and brother.”

The makers of Gen V, The Boys spin-off series said the 27-year-old said: “We can’t quite wrap our heads around this.”

Chance was always charming and smiling, an enthusiastic force of nature, an incredibly talented performer, and more than anything else, just a very kind, lovely person.”

“Just writing about him in the past tense doesn’t make sense,” they emotionally added.

“We are so sorry for Chance’s family, and we are grieving the loss of our friend and colleague. Hug your loved ones tonight.”

Authorities have advised that no other individuals were involved in the accident.

The British-American actor shared his last Instagram post just three days ago where he was all smiles in the gym. “Haven’t started. Already exhausted. Whatever it takes, I guess,” he wrote in the caption.

On March 17, the rapper posted a photo of his new bike within a photo dump, alongside the caption, “Day 77. Final calm before storm #backontheroad.”

Tributes pour in for the TV star

As the tragic news broke, fans of the young star took to X, previously known as Twitter to share their grief and condolences.

“This was such an unjust end to a career that was just beginning. Rip #ChancePerdomo, you gave us such memorable characters in such a short while. Thank you,” penned one fan.

“Heartbroken. LOVE YOU MAN ❤❤❤ RIP!” Arnold Schwarzenegger‘s son Patrick commented on his last Instagram post.

“Rest in peace. You were super damn talented and we were lucky to witness your talent,” penned the artist Michael James Schneider.

One fan wrote: “RIP Chance Perdomo. A young talent gone way too soon.”
