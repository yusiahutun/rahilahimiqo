UK's most wanted murder suspects from earless pub shooter to gangland enforcer, 'Mr Big'

February 19, 2024
blog

A gangland's 'Mr Big,' a pub shooter and a suspected double murderer are on the UK's most wanted list.

Brits have been urged to keep their eyes peeled for the fugitives who have all been on the run for over 10 years. The National Crime Agency are hunting the three suspects believed to be connected to grim murders.

If spotted, the agency has warned people not to approach them and to instead dial 999.

The NCA said: "We pursue criminals and bring them to justice. By informing communities and supporting other agencies, we can help identify criminals and speed the law enforcement process."

Kevin Thomas Parle - also known as 'Hemp' - is wanted in connection with the shooting murders of 16-year-old Liam Kelly in 2004 and Lucy Hargreaves, 22 in 2005 in Liverpool.

Over recent years Peter Bleksley, a former Metropolitan police undercover detective, has led an investigation into Parle's whereabouts. The fugituve has beebn on the run for over 15 years.

In December, 2022, Bleksley told the Liverpool Echo: "I think there are two distinct camps or schools of thought on this. The first is that Parle is very much alive and well, and probably living in Spain...The second camp believe that Parle was murdered many years ago, and is ' long gone.' Many individuals in the prison system believe this to be true."

Over recent years Mr Bleksley has claimed to have received credible information that Parle has been protected by a sophisticated criminal network. The trained investigator has reported that 6ft 6" Parle, of heavy build, has spent time in Spain, Dubai and Thailand.

In an episode of his BBC podcast series Manhunt: Finding Kevin Parle, Mr Bleksley discusses tip offs that suggested Parle worked as an enforcer for a major gang based in Spain. That outfit is described as a drugs network with access to vast sums of money potentially laundered by a man referred to by Mr Bleksley as ‘Ian’.

Liam Kelly was just 16 when he was shot dead on Grafton Street, Dingle, in 2004. Parle was questioned in connection with his murder but was released on bail - which he failed to answer.

He is the subject of a European Arrest Warrant in relation to that investigation.

The following year 22-year-old Lucy Hargreaves was shot as she slept on a living room sofa in her Walton home. Petrol was then poured around the room and set alight.

Detectives want to speak to Parle, formerly of Mossley Hill, as part of inquiries into the mum’s killing, believed to have been a case of mistaken identity.

Allan Foster

Allan Foster is alleged to have murdered father-of-seven, David 'Noddy' Rice in a gangland killing.

The 42-year-old was was shot dead in a seafront car park at Marsden Grotto, South Shields, on 24 May 2006. One man was jailed for murder, but police are still hunting suspect Allan Foster, who is believed to be in Spain.

Mr Rice, who was a known criminal, was shot nine times after he was lured to the car park on the pretence of discussing a debt.

Foster, now aged in his mid-40s, is also wanted for two offences of conspiring to supply controlled drugs and also for the theft of a diamond ring.

Police previously referred to him as "the Mr Big behind the killing" and launched an international appeal to locate him in 2008.

Foster has links to the Canary Islands and Majorca. He is also known to use the alias Shaun Michael Wilkinson.

Derek McGraw Ferguson

Police are still on the hunt for Derek McGraw Ferguson, 59, over the death of Thomas Cameron, 49, who was shot dead outside The Auchinairn Tavern in Bishopbriggs on 28 June 2007.

Officers said Mr Cameron left the pub to speak to two men, but just a few minutes later one of them pulled out a gun and shot him.

Det Ch Supt Paul Livingstone described the murder as a "horrific crime" and said his officers remained determined to solve the case.

He added: "Our extensive inquiries have never ceased, which underlines our unwavering commitment to tracking down Derek Ferguson.

"We are reiterating our appeal for anyone who has any knowledge of his whereabouts to get in touch with any information which could help us trace him."

Intelligence gathered suggests Ferguson may have travelled between the southern part of the Netherlands and Spain including Barcelona, the south coast and the Balearic Islands.

EvoFIT images have since been released showing what he might look like now.

Ferguson is described as being between 5ft 2in and 5ft 6in in height. He has a small scar on his right jawline, a small piece missing from the top of his left ear, and was known to have various tattoos on his forearms, including a heart and a dagger.

Ferguson, who is in his late 50s, is unlikely to be known by his original name.

Police have also said he may no longer speak with a Scottish accent.

A reward of up to £10,000 is on offer from Crimestoppers for information that leads to the arrest of Ferguson.

Anyone with any information about the suspects is urged to call the National Crime Agency on 0370 496 7622.
