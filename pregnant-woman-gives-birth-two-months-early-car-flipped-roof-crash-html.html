Pregnant Brittany Beaudoin gives birth two months early after car crash

September 19, 2024
blog

Brittany Beaudoin with her husband Denis and new baby Aiden. She was forced to give birth after her car flipped over 

A pregnant mother was forced to give birth two months early after the car she was travelling in was run off the road and flipped on to its roof.

But miraculously both mother and baby survived even though the infant had to be delievered at eight months.

Brittany Beaudoin from New Hampshire had been to a doctor's appointment with her husband Denis when their car clipped a pole, causing their vehicle to be thrown in the air.

Both were thrown from the car but luckily the pair survived and they were rushed to hospital where Ms Beaudoin was told she would have to give birth.

Doctors performed an emergency C-section and they welcomed their son, Aiden two months early.

And despite having to be helped with his breathing, the infant is doing well while his parents recover from their injuries, which included broken bones and dislocations.

Now having seen the CCTV footage, which shows the moment of impact of the crash, Ms Beaudoin father Eric Turner, says he can't believe his daughter and son-in-law only walked away with broken bones.

He told CBS Boston: 'Watching that surveillance, it does look like he was chasing another car in a race, trying to catch them. I just can’t imagine racing on these streets out here.

'Denis just said there was a car that pushed him off the road and into a pole. He said it was like the car was coming at him faster for some reason instead of trying to stop.

Now the family are hoping they will br allowed back to their home in Manchester and are preparing to celebrate Christmas, which would have been Aiden's original due date.

CCTV footage shows the moment the car was flipped on its roof after it was forced off the road and hit a pole 

The other driver involved in the crash has not been charged.

Officials at Elliot Hospital in Manchester said Mrs Beaudoin is in 'fair' condition, but could not give out more details.

Now the family has set up a GoFundMe page to help pay for the medical costs towards their treatment.
