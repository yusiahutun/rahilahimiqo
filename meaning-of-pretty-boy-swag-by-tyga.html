Meaning of Pretty Boy Swag by Tyga

February 02, 2024
blog

In the world of music, there are songs that touch on deep emotions, songs that make you dance, and then there are songs like Tyga's "Pretty Boy Swag" that embrace the confidence and swagger that comes with being at the top. This track is all about Tyga's self-assuredness and his ability to showcase his dominance in the game. While the lyrics may seem straightforward at first, there are hidden messages and underlying themes that deserve a closer look.One standout lyric in the song is "Bitch I'm bigger, you small, I be clowin' calls, And your just a funny guy, little duval." This lyric not only showcases Tyga's witty wordplay but also highlights the theme of power dynamics. Tyga asserts his superiority over others, emphasizing his larger-than-life presence in the industry. By comparing himself to Little Duval, a comedian known for his hilarious antics, Tyga implies that he's the one pulling the strings behind the scenes, controlling the narrative and making people laugh.Moving further into the song, we stumble upon the line "Nigga damn, always talkin' to bitches not my age, Yea Im young, you old, so put these babies on yo face." This lyric touches on the theme of age and the bravado that comes with youth. Tyga isn't afraid to point out the generational gap, reminding his older counterparts that they are no match for his youthful energy. The mention of putting "babies" on their face adds a cheeky touch, suggesting that Tyga's success and dominance have achieved a level of immaturity and playfulness.Another phrase that stands out is "Treat my bitches like they sisters, Now you work for me." At first glance, this line may seem derogatory, but when interpreted metaphorically, it reveals a theme of loyalty and camaraderie. Tyga sees his team as his family, knowing that they have his back no matter what. They work together to build empires and achieve greatness. In this context, calling them "bitches" is not derogatory but rather a term of endearment, suggesting a deep bond and understanding among the group.As the song progresses, Tyga drops lines like "Spazz man, All about gettin' my cash fast, Im totally rad, show her my tats." These lyrics hint at the importance of self-expression and individuality. Tyga believes in going all out, being unapologetically himself, and embracing his "rad" qualities. By showing off his tattoos, he embodies the idea that art is an extension of oneself, and through this visual medium, he illustrates his confidence and uniqueness. The theme here lies in the celebration of personal style and identity.Moving on, Tyga exclaims, "I got models, I got friends, But I never spend on them." This lyric delves into the theme of materialism and the complexities of relationships built on superficiality. Tyga, being a successful artist, is surrounded by the glitz and glamour of the entertainment industry. However, he subtly suggests that true connections cannot be bought or fabricated. While he may have access to "models" and "friends," he understands that genuine relationships go beyond materialistic gestures.Lastly, we encounter the line "Only circle that I'm in is the circle of success, Fuck stress, have sex, And I do that shit the best." This part of the song highlights the theme of the pursuit of excellence. Tyga acknowledges that success comes with its own set of challenges and stresses. However, he chooses to rise above the pressure by focusing on what brings him joy and pleasure. By linking success, the absence of stress, and sexual prowess, Tyga depicts a carefree mindset and a belief in his ability to conquer any obstacle that comes his way.Although "Pretty Boy Swag" may appear as a straightforward song about Tyga's confidence and dominance, delving deeper into the lyrics uncovers unexpected and imaginative themes. From power dynamics and age to loyalty and individuality, Tyga's words paint a picture of a man fully immersed in his own greatness. The song becomes more than just a boastful anthem; it becomes a reflection of the multifaceted aspects of success and the identity that comes with it.
