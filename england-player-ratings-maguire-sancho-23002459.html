England player ratings as Maguire, Sancho and Calvert-Lewin net vs Ireland

blog

England made light work of the Republic of Ireland this evening, cruising to a 3-0 win over Stephen Kelly's side at Wembley.

It was a performance which saw Gareth Southgate's side hardly get out of first gear, controlling proceedings throughout.

Harry Maguire, wearing the armband, netted first - rising above Shane Duffy to head home in the first half.

Jadon Sancho made it two soon after, scoring a well-taken goal after being set up by Jack Grealish.

And Dominic Calvert-Lewin made it three, scoring from the spot in the second-half.

But who impressed for England this evening?

Nick POPE 6 - Little to do so little chance to impress. Question marks remain when the ball is at his feet.

Michael KEANE 7 - Looks more and more comfortable in an England shirt. Made huge strides

HARRY MAGUIRE 8 - Wore the captain’s armband, scored the opener and bossed his back three

Mason MOUNT 7 - Asked to play in central midfield but did not look out of place at all. Industrious

Bukayo SAKA 7 - Got forward well, should have scored, and won a penalty. Breakthrough performance.

Jadon SANCHO 7 - Scored a belter and could have added to it. Picked up some great positions.

Dominic CALVERT-LEWIN 7 - Smashed home his penalty. A tireless runner. Worked so hard for the team.

Jack GREALISH 7 - Buzzed around picking passes and opening doors for his team-mates. Growing in stature.
