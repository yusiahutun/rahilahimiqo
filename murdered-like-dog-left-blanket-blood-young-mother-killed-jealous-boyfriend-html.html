A 21-year-old South Florida man is due in court today in Miami accused of killing his 17-year-old girlfriend like a 'dog' and wrapping her bloodied body in a white blanket.

Hialeah Police arrested Yoan Raul Dominguez on Saturday and charged him with second-degree murder for the shooting of his girlfriend, Genesis Fonseca at her family home during a get-together.

Fonseca's cousin, David Lopez said that her sister and her mother left to get some food at 8 p.m. on Saturday and returned to find her 'wrapped like a dog'. 'And murdered like a dog. She was found in a white blanket full of blood.'

Dominguez us currently being held without bond by Miami-Dade Corrections and has a long prior list of convictions, including an open case for aggravated battery against an elderly person last month.

He and Fonseca had been seeing each other for only a few months and asked her mother if she could bring Dominguez to a family party on Saturday.

At around 8 p.m. 'My mom left with her boyfriend and went to go get them food,' said Fonseca's sister, Macraso to WSVN-TV.Com.

'When she came back. My mom said that the door was locked. She couldn't open it and nobody was answering.'

Genesis Fonseca, 17, was preparing to celebrate the 1st birthday of her baby boy (right)

Dominguez was arrested in Miami on Sunday - one day after he was seen fleeing the scene of the crime in Hialeah, Florida on Saturday

When they walked inside, they gound a gruesome scene: 'When we walked in we just saw blood on the floor and we were like, 'What's going on?'.

'There was also blood on the couch but she didn't notice that her body was in front of the couch.'

Fonseca has a young child who will celebrate his first birthday this week.

Once the family realised that Dominguez had fled they phoned for the police who arrested him in Miami at around 7 a.m. on Sunday.

Genesis Fonseca was murdered in her mother's Hialeah home while she waited for food to arrive

Police said Dominguez was seen running from the apartment.

'He was a jealous guy from what I had heard,' said Macraso to WSVN-TV.Com. 'I mean, I didn't think he would do something like that.'

Other family members hoped that Dominguez would answer for his crimes.

'Justice will be served. The same way my family is crying and feeling it, I feel bad for his family, I understand they're probably out there crying too,' Lopez said.
