Gwyneth Paltrow's Son Is Chris Martin's Lookalike in New Pic

June 01, 2024
blog

Gwyneth Paltrow and Chris Martin's son Moses is all grown up — and he's looking more and more like his father. While his sister Apple is certainly their mother's doppelgänger, Paltrow just posted a rare image with Moses that proves he is the spitting image of the Coldplay front man.

On Thursday, Paltrow took to Instagram Stories for a little round of "Ask me a question" (a favorite pastime of hers), and one fan requested to see her favorite photo of her with her son. Paltrow posted the sweetest selfie capturing her leaning on her son's shoulders (who also appears to have hit a growth spurt). Moses is wearing a black sweater layered over a white tee, accessorized with a silver pendant chain and matching tiny hoop earrings. For her part, Paltrow is going makeup-free while wearing a gray-and-white zip-up sweater.

Both Paltrow and Moses give a soft smile for the camera, and Moses looks like he could be his father's twin. "Not fave but recent," she captioned the picture with a pink double-heart emoji.

It's not often that Paltrow shares images of Moses. However, in December, the Goop founder shared a gallery of images from her family's trip to Italy. Moses can be seen lounging on a boat alongside his sister, as well as sitting on the edge of an infinity pool. Paltrow also gave fans a glimpse into their Christmas festivities with an Instagram post containing two selfies with both of her kids at the holidays.

Paltrow and Martin have previously been very open about their successful co-parenting relationship. In an interview last year, Paltrow said that Martin "sprinkles fairy dust" when he's around the kids.

“He has this sweet, child-like spirit, and I love that he will be like, 'Let's go jump in the ocean,’ when it's 58 degrees,” she told People. “Or, 'Let's go to this weird guitar store.' He always wants to do these fun adventures with them and activities.”

She added that her ex-husband “has a real sweetness. And so he brings that to the way that he parents them ... He's sort of gone a lot, he's on the road a lot, but he brings — it's almost like he comes and sprinkles fairy dust.”
